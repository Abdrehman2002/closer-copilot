# Web Design & Development — a site that turns visitors into customers

## What we sell & the transformation
We design and build a fast, modern website (WordPress, Shopify, or custom) that actually converts — clear offer, strong calls to action, mobile-first, built to be found. Then a monthly care plan keeps it fast, secure, backed up, and updated so it never breaks. The transformation: from an embarrassing or dead site that loses trust in 3 seconds, to a site they're proud to send people to — one that books calls and takes orders while they sleep.

## Ideal buyer & how to read them
A business owner whose current site is old, slow, DIY, or nonexistent — service businesses, local shops, e-commerce, coaches, professionals. They know their site is holding them back but dread the hassle. Read them: if they wince describing their current site, sell the relief of "done for you, live in [2 weeks]"; if they hesitate on price, tie it to what one lost customer or one deal costs them.

## Core pain & the cost of doing nothing (in their numbers)
- Visitors judge a site in about 3 seconds; a slow, dated, or clunky site sends them straight to a competitor and quietly kills trust before a word is read.
- More than half of web traffic is mobile — if it's not clean on a phone, most visitors are gone.
- Doing nothing has a running cost: every day the site leaks the leads and sales the marketing worked to earn.
- Math to run live: "How many people hit your site a month? If even a handful more became customers because it actually worked, at $[X] each that's real money you're losing right now."

## Objection playbook
### "Why is it so expensive, I've seen sites for way cheaper"
- Move: separate a cheap template from a site built to make money
- Say: "Totally — you can get a $200 template. But that's a brochure you have to figure out and it won't convert. We build the thing that turns visitors into booked calls and sales, and we handle all of it. One extra customer a month usually pays for the whole build. What's a customer worth to you?"
### "My nephew / a friend can build it for cheap"
- Move: reframe from 'built' to 'built to work and maintained'
- Say: "They probably can put something up. The question is whether it loads fast, ranks, converts, and doesn't break in six months when they're busy. We build it to sell and we keep it running so it's never your problem. That's the difference between a site and a salesperson."
### "I already have a website"
- Move: shift from 'have one' to 'is it earning its keep'
- Say: "Perfect — so let me ask: when did it last actually bring you a customer? If it's slow, dated, or invisible on a phone, it's costing you quietly. Want me to do a quick teardown and show you exactly where it's leaking?"
### "You're overseas, how do I know the quality"
- Move: address it directly and let the work answer
- Say: "Fair — our team's in Pakistan, which is why you get senior design at a price a US shop can't match. But don't take my word for it: let me show you sites we've built, and we work in stages you approve as we go, so you see the quality before you're all-in."
### "How does communication work with the timezone"
- Move: concrete cadence over reassurance
- Say: "We work your hours. You get a shared project board where you see progress live, a check-in at a time that suits you, and same-day replies. You'll never be wondering what's happening — that's the part cheap builders get wrong."
### "I've been burned by a web guy who took my money and disappeared"
- Move: de-risk with milestones and ownership
- Say: "Yeah, that's way too common. So we don't do that — we work in milestones you sign off on, you own the site and all the logins from day one, and there's a clear timeline. You're never trusting me with a big check and hoping."
### "It's not a good time / let me think about it"
- Move: isolate and shrink the commitment
- Say: "Totally fair. Just so I follow up right — is it the timing, the budget, or wanting to see more of our work? Whichever it is, I can usually sort it in five minutes."

## Proof & risk-reversal
- Show the portfolio and, ideally, a quick live teardown of THEIR current site — nothing sells like seeing their own leaks.
- Milestone-based payments and you own everything from day one. Offer real revisions in writing. Never invent past results or client names.

## Voice & phrases
Confident, visual, reassuring — you're removing a headache. Always: "done for you," "live in [X], you approve every step," "it works while you sleep." Never: overpromise a launch date you can't hit, and never use developer jargon on an owner.
