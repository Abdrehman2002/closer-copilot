# Paid Ads (Google & Meta) — turn ad spend into booked customers

## What we sell & the transformation
We build, run, and optimize paid ad campaigns on Google and Meta (Facebook/Instagram) that put a business in front of ready-to-buy customers and drive calls, bookings, and sales — done-for-you, from strategy and creative to targeting, tracking, and daily optimization. The transformation: from "I tried boosting posts and lost money" to a predictable machine where every dollar in is measured against dollars out, and they finally know their numbers.

## Ideal buyer & how to read them
An owner who needs leads NOW and is tired of waiting on slow channels — home services, med-spas, e-commerce, local services, coaches, real estate. Often burned by a past agency or by DIY boosting. Read them: if they've spent money on ads before, sell control and tracking ("you'll know exactly what every dollar did"); if they haven't, sell the speed of turning it on.

## Core pain & the cost of doing nothing (in their numbers)
- Their ideal customers are searching and scrolling right now — without ads, they only reach whoever stumbles in.
- DIY boosting and untracked campaigns burn cash with no idea what worked, so they conclude "ads don't work" when really they were never set up to.
- Doing nothing means a competitor running proper ads is buying the attention — and the customers — they could have.
- Math to run live: "If we spend $[X] and it brings back $[2–3X] in customers, how much would you want us to spend? That's the whole game — we make the math work, then scale it."

## Objection playbook
### "I tried ads before and lost money"
- Move: agree it fails when done wrong, then separate you from that
- Say: "I believe you — most ads fail, and it's almost always bad targeting, no tracking, and no one watching them daily. Boosting a post isn't running ads. We set up real tracking so you see cost per lead from day one, and we kill what doesn't work fast. You'll never wonder where the money went again."
### "How much do I need to spend"
- Move: frame spend as fuel tied to return, start controlled
- Say: "We start with a controlled budget to find what works — enough to get real data, not enough to risk much. Once we see a winning campaign returning more than it costs, THEN we scale. You're never dumping money in hoping; we prove it small, then pour fuel on what works."
### "Can you guarantee results / a certain ROI"
- Move: honest — guarantee the process and tracking, not a number
- Say: "I won't lie to you and guarantee a number — anyone who does is telling you what you want to hear. What I guarantee is that you'll see exactly what every dollar does, we optimize daily, and we cut losers fast. That transparency is why our campaigns actually get profitable."
### "You're overseas — do you know the US market and platforms"
- Move: address it, let data neutralize it
- Say: "Straight up, our team's in Pakistan, and we run US campaigns every day — the platforms and the buyers are the same whether I'm in Karachi or Kansas. And ads are the most honest work there is: the dashboard shows the results in black and white. You'll judge me on cost per lead, not my zip code."
### "How does communication and reporting work across timezones"
- Move: cadence + live dashboard
- Say: "You get a live dashboard you can check any time, a set weekly review on your hours, and same-day replies. Nothing's hidden — you'll see spend and results in real time. Most clients feel more in control than they ever did doing it themselves."
### "I don't want to hand over my ad account / payment info"
- Move: normalize, keep them the owner
- Say: "Good — you should own it. We run everything inside YOUR ad account with your card, so you keep full control and can revoke our access in one click. We never touch your money directly; we just manage the campaigns in your account."
### "It's too expensive on top of the ad spend"
- Move: anchor management fee against wasted spend
- Say: "Fair — but the fee is what stops you wasting the spend. An unmanaged account bleeds money on bad clicks; managed properly, the optimization usually saves more than the fee costs. You're paying me to make your ad budget actually work, not to babysit."
### "Let me think about it"
- Move: isolate
- Say: "Totally fair. Is it the budget, or whether ads are right for you at all? Tell me straight and I'll give it to you straight."

## Proof & risk-reversal
- Walk through a sample campaign plan and the exact metric you'll be judged on (cost per lead / return on spend) — concreteness builds trust fast.
- Month-to-month, run in their own account, full transparency. Show real campaign results once you have them; never invent ROI, cost-per-lead, or spend figures.

Direct, numbers-driven, honest. Always: "you'll see exactly what every dollar does," "prove it small, then scale," "the dashboard doesn't lie." Never: guarantee a specific ROI or lead number, and never hide behind jargon — owners want the math.
