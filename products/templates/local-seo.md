# Local SEO — get found by customers already searching

## What we sell & the transformation
We get a local business ranking in the Google Map Pack and local search, so the customers already typing "plumber near me" or "[service] [city]" find THEM first — not the competitor three blocks over. Done-for-you: Google Business Profile optimization, local citations, on-page, a reviews engine, and monthly content. They touch nothing. The transformation: from invisible on page 3 to the first name a ready-to-buy local customer calls.

## Ideal buyer & how to read them
Owner-operator of a local service business — home services, dental, legal, med-spa, auto, restaurants — doing $300k–3M a year. Practical, busy, allergic to jargon, usually burned by a "marketing guy" before. Read them: if they lean in at "customers searching for you right now," they feel the pain; if they go quiet at price, anchor on one customer's value before you name a number.

## Core pain & the cost of doing nothing (in their numbers)
- Nearly half of Google searches are local, and "near me" searches convert fast because the person is ready to buy NOW.
- If they're not in the top 3 map results, they're invisible to most of that traffic — those calls go to whoever ranks.
- Doing nothing isn't neutral: every month unranked, the competitor above them compounds reviews and authority, and the gap widens.
- Math to run live: "How many new customers a month would move the needle? If even 5 more find you and each is worth $[X], that's $[5X] a month walking to someone else."

## Objection playbook
### "SEO takes too long, I need customers now"
- Move: reframe the timeline as a compounding asset and set an early-win expectation
- Say: "Fair — that's exactly why we start with your Google profile and reviews, which move in weeks, not months. Rankings compound after that. But would you rather be ranking in 90 days, or still invisible in 90 days having done nothing?"
### "I've been burned by an SEO company before"
- Move: stop selling the service, start selling trust and transparency
- Say: "I hear that constantly, and honestly it's why people are skeptical of us too. So here's the deal — you get a monthly report showing exactly what we did and where you rank, and it's month-to-month. If it's not working, you walk. The last guy locked you in; I won't."
### "Is SEO even worth it now with AI and ChatGPT"
- Move: separate discovery from the decision
- Say: "Good question. When someone needs a dentist, AI might tell them what to look for — but they still Google 'dentist near me,' read your reviews, and book. AI sends them looking; your ranking is where they actually pick you. That's not going away."
### "You're overseas, where are you based"
- Move: address it head-on and turn the cost structure into their advantage
- Say: "Straight up — our team's in Pakistan, and that's exactly why you get senior work at a price a US agency can't touch. You deal with me directly, on your hours, and I'll jump on a call any time. You're not paying for a fancy office — you're paying for rankings."
### "How do I know you'll actually communicate, what about timezone"
- Move: give a concrete cadence, not a reassurance
- Say: "Here's the cadence: a set weekly check-in at a time that works for YOU, a monthly report, and I answer same-day. I work your hours, not mine. You'll hear from me more than the last agency did — that's a promise."
### "I don't want to hand over access to my website or Google account"
- Move: normalize it and lower the stake
- Say: "Totally reasonable. You stay the owner of everything — I get manager access you can revoke in one click, and I'll walk you through exactly what I touch. Your accounts stay yours."
### "It's too expensive"
- Move: tie the price to one customer's value, not the monthly number
- Say: "Compared to what one new customer is worth to you? You said each one's about $[X]. This pays for itself the month it brings you one or two. It's not a cost — it's how you get found by people ready to spend."
### "Let me think about it"
- Move: isolate the real objection
- Say: "Totally fair. Just so I know what to follow up on — is it the price, or whether we can actually get you ranking?"

## Proof & risk-reversal
- Lead with the audit: show them exactly where they rank today versus the top competitor — it's undeniable and specific to them.
- Month-to-month, cancel anytime. Use real client results and screenshots once you have them — never invent rankings, traffic, or numbers.

Calm, plain-spoken, zero jargon — talk like a local, not a marketer. Always: "customers searching for you right now," "found first," "the risk's on me." Never: "algorithm," "synergy," or "guaranteed #1 ranking" — never promise a specific ranking.
