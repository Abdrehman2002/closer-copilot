# Social Media Management — show up daily so they stay top of mind

## What we sell & the transformation
We run a brand's social media end-to-end — strategy, content, posting, captions, engagement, and monthly reporting across the platforms that matter for them (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn). Done-for-you: they stop scrambling to post and start showing up consistently, on-brand, every day. The transformation: from a dead, embarrassing feed that makes the business look inactive, to a steady presence that builds trust, keeps them top of mind, and turns followers into customers.

## Ideal buyer & how to read them
An owner or marketing lead who knows they "should" be posting but has no time, no consistency, and a feed that's gone quiet — local brands, e-commerce, restaurants, gyms, coaches, service businesses. Read them: if they're embarrassed about their feed, sell relief and consistency; if they doubt ROI, tie social to trust and being remembered, not vanity metrics.

## Core pain & the cost of doing nothing (in their numbers)
- Customers check social before they buy — a dead or last-posted-8-months-ago feed reads as "is this business even still open?"
- Posting sporadically themselves eats hours and still looks inconsistent, so it never compounds.
- Doing nothing means every day a competitor with an active, trusted feed wins the attention and the sale.
- Frame to run live: "When someone hears about you, the first thing they do is check your page. What does yours say about you right now — and what's that costing you in customers who quietly move on?"

## Objection playbook
### "I can just post myself"
- Move: acknowledge it, then surface the real cost — consistency and time
- Say: "You absolutely can — the issue is never the first week, it's week six when you're slammed and the feed goes quiet again. That inconsistency is what kills it. We make sure it never stops, on-brand, without you thinking about it. What's your time worth per hour?"
### "Does social media actually drive sales"
- Move: reframe from direct sales to trust and top-of-mind
- Say: "Fair question. Social rarely closes the sale on its own — it's what makes people trust you and remember you so that when they're ready, you're the obvious choice. It's the difference between 'never heard of them' and 'oh yeah, I follow them.' That's worth real money."
### "Which platforms do I even need"
- Move: consultative — fewer, done well
- Say: "Honestly, not all of them — that's a trap. Let's pick the one or two where your customers actually are and dominate those instead of spreading thin. For your business I'd bet on [platform]. Better to own one than be mediocre on five."
### "You're overseas, will you get my brand and my market"
- Move: address it and make process the answer
- Say: "Straight up, our team's in Pakistan — and we work US brands every day. We start with a brand kit and a content plan you approve before anything goes live, so your voice is locked in first. You'll sign off on the direction; nothing goes out that isn't you."
### "How does communication work with the timezone"
- Move: concrete cadence
- Say: "You get a monthly content calendar to approve in advance, a set check-in on your hours, and same-day replies. You see everything before it posts. You'll be more in control than you are posting it yourself."
### "I've been burned by an agency that just posted random stuff"
- Move: de-risk with approval and a plan
- Say: "Yeah, the 'post-and-pray' agencies give everyone a bad name. We don't post a thing you haven't seen — you approve the monthly plan up front, it ties to an actual goal, and it's month-to-month. If it's not working, you walk."
### "It's too expensive for social media"
- Move: anchor against a hire and against lost trust
- Say: "Compared to what — hiring someone in-house at a few grand a month, or the customers who check your dead page and move on? This is a fraction of a hire, fully handled, and it's the face of your business online. What's one lost customer a week worth?"
### "Let me think about it"
- Move: isolate
- Say: "Totally fair — is it the budget, or whether social's the right focus for you right now? Tell me which and I'll be straight with you."

## Proof & risk-reversal
- Show sample content and a mini content plan built for THEIR brand — seeing their own feed reimagined sells it.
- Month-to-month, approve everything before it posts. Show real results and handles once you have them; never fabricate follower or engagement numbers.

## Competition & why us (include 'doing nothing')
- vs an in-house hire: a fraction of the salary, a whole team's skills, no management headache.
- vs a cheap freelancer: strategy and consistency, not random posts — and you approve the plan.
- vs doing nothing: a stale feed actively costs you trust every time a potential customer checks it.

## The close
Book it on the call. Anchor: "$900 a month, three platforms handled, first content calendar within a week — month-to-month." (Swap in your own price.) Assumptive: "Want me to build your first content plan and get you posting this week?" Get the start commitment before it drifts to "let me check with my partner."

## Voice & phrases
Warm, creative, confident. Always: "we handle it, you approve it," "consistency is the whole game," "you're the face — let's make it look like it." Never: promise viral results or specific follower counts, and never talk down to someone who's been posting themselves.
