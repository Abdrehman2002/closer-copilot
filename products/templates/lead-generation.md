# Lead Generation & Appointment Setting — booked sales calls, done for you

## What we sell & the transformation
We fill a business's calendar with qualified sales appointments — done-for-you cold outreach (cold email, LinkedIn, and/or cold calling) that finds their ideal customers, starts the conversation, handles the back-and-forth, and books interested prospects straight onto their calendar. The transformation: from an empty pipeline and "where's my next client coming from," to waking up to booked calls with people who want to talk — so they can do what they're good at: closing.

## Ideal buyer & how to read them
A B2B service business, agency, consultant, coach, or contractor whose growth is capped by not enough conversations — they can close, they just don't have enough at-bats. Read them: if they light up at "booked calls on your calendar," they feel the pipeline pain; if they doubt lead quality, sell the qualification and the pay-for-results framing.

## Core pain & the cost of doing nothing (in their numbers)
- Their revenue is capped by pipeline: no matter how good they close, they can only close conversations they actually have.
- Doing outreach themselves is the first thing that falls off when they get busy delivering — so pipeline goes feast-or-famine.
- Doing nothing means the slow months keep coming, and every empty calendar slot is a deal that never got the chance to happen.
- Math to run live: "If we booked you even [8–10] qualified calls a month and you close one in [three or four], what's a client worth to you over its lifetime? That's the number this is really about."

## Objection playbook
### "I've tried cold email / callers before and it didn't work"
- Move: agree most outreach is bad, separate you on targeting and qualification
- Say: "I get it — most outreach is spray-and-pray to the wrong people with a weak message, so it flops. We start with exactly who your best customer is, write messaging that speaks to their actual problem, and only book calls with people who've shown real interest. You're not getting volume — you're getting the right conversations."
### "How do I know the leads/appointments will be qualified"
- Move: define 'qualified' on their terms, up front
- Say: "Great — let's define it together right now so we're aligned: the right industry, the right size, a real need, and they agreed to the call. We don't book tire-kickers, because a no-show wastes your time and mine. You tell me what a perfect prospect looks like and that's exactly who lands on your calendar."
### "Can you do pay-per-appointment / only pay for results"
- Move: acknowledge the instinct, steer to a fair structure
- Say: "I like that you think in results — so do I. Here's the honest version: the best setup is a base that lets us run consistent, quality outreach, tied to a real booked-call target so you know what you're getting. Pure pay-per-lead pushes everyone toward junk leads to hit a number, and you don't want that. Let's tie it to qualified calls, not just any calls."
### "You're overseas — will the outreach sound off to US prospects"
- Move: address it, make the written/scripted work the answer
- Say: "Straight up, our team's in Pakistan. But the outreach goes out in your name, in clean US English, and you approve the messaging and scripts before a single message goes out. Your prospects experience your brand, not our location. And you'll see every message — nothing goes out that isn't sharp."
### "How does communication and reporting work across timezones"
- Move: concrete cadence + visibility
- Say: "You get a shared tracker showing outreach sent, replies, and calls booked, a set weekly review on your hours, and same-day replies from me. You'll always know exactly how the pipeline's filling — no black box."
### "I don't want you contacting people as my company without control"
- Move: reassure with approval gates
- Say: "You should be in control — you approve the target list and the messaging before we send, everything goes out in a way you've signed off on, and you see the replies. Your reputation is the whole point; we protect it like it's ours."
### "It's too expensive / not sure it's worth it"
- Move: anchor against one client's lifetime value
- Say: "Fair — so what's one new client worth to you over a year? If this brings you even one or two you wouldn't have had, it's paid for itself many times over. You're not buying messages sent; you're buying booked calls with people who want what you do."
### "Let me think about it"
- Move: isolate
- Say: "Totally fair. Is it the investment, or whether the appointments will actually be quality? Tell me which and I'll be straight with you about it."

## Proof & risk-reversal
- Walk them through the exact target profile and a sample outreach message written for THEIR offer — seeing a real message to a real prospect makes it concrete.
- Approval on list and messaging, a clear monthly booked-call target, month-to-month. Show real booked-call results once you have them; never invent appointment or close-rate numbers.

## Competition & why us (include 'doing nothing')
- vs an in-house SDR: a fraction of the salary, no hiring or training, running from day one.
- vs a cheap lead list: we start real conversations and book qualified calls, not a spreadsheet of stale contacts.
- vs doing nothing: the pipeline stays feast-or-famine, and the slow months keep coming.

## The close
Book it on the call. Anchor: "$1,500 a month, targeting a set number of qualified booked calls, first outreach live within a week — month-to-month." (Swap in your own price and target.) Assumptive: "Want me to build your target list and get outreach going this week?" Get the start commitment before it drifts to "let me see how things look next month."

## Voice & phrases
Confident, straight-talking, results-oriented. Always: "qualified calls on your calendar," "you approve everything before it goes out," "you focus on closing, we'll fill the pipeline." Never: promise a specific number of closed deals, and never sell volume over quality — this buyer's been burned by junk leads before.
